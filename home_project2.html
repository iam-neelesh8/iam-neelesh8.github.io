Project Name : Analyzing Employee Attrition

Team : Sri Manogna Gurijala , Neelesh Reddypelli.

Attrition is the departure of employees from an organization for any reason, including termination,death or retirement. We have chosen HR Employees attritions data from kaggle.

Problem Statement:

Attrition is one common concern that any organization has and always wanted to take care of the numbers in terms of the employees to retain them in the organization. With all the parameters present in the Dataset, our goal is to specify the factors that are affecting an organization in terms of its employee attrition. One such problem is the focus of Project 2. The data set is designed and collected to know the reasons behind the departure of the employees from the company for certain reasons. It is a vast dataset that has both Numerical and categorical values in it.

Software Used: Tableau Desktop

Dataset Background:

The data set consists of 35 different features with 1470 rows From 35 different features we have 15 Numerical columns and 20 categorical columns

Categorical Columns:

Some of the Major column features and their explanation:

'Age': The Age of the Employee.

'DistanceFromHome': Distance the employee lives from home.

'EmployeeNumber': Employee ID
'EnvironmentSatisfaction': Contains 4 different values – Low, Medium, High, Very high

Questions:

Our Major goal is to find the reasons for employee attrition through our visualization. With the dataset we have and the major columns in it, we thought there are certain factors that can be major reasons for employee attrition in the organization. We as a team had an initial draft of how we can put our thoughts into visualization using Tableau and create interactivity using those visualizations.

Detailed explanation for questions with the answers :
1.What is the Total attrition count?

Through our visualization, with the given records in the data, we have found that there are a total of 1470 employee records in the data out of which 1233 have not left the organization in a certain period whereas 237 employees have left the organization. To make sure our dashboard is simple and easy to understand for every person, we have included the up and down arrows to make it visually simple.

2.What is the effect of Job satisfaction on attrition? Does Job satisfaction lead to attrition?

From our understanding, we can see that JOB SATISFACTION plays a major role in attrition.

As attrition is the dependent variable it should be in the y-axis and job satisfaction being the independent variable should be on the x-axis. In the below Visualization, we can notice that people have been categorized into 4 different satisfaction levels ( Not satisfied, Neutral, Satisfied, and Extremely Satisfied). From the observation, it is clear that there is a high attrition rate for the people who are not satisfied with their job and also who are neutral with their opinion.
